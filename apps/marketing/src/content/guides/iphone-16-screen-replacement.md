## Overview

The iPhone 16 uses Apple's Ceramic Shield glass bonded to an OLED display module with
IP68 waterproof adhesive. This guide covers a full front assembly (LCD + frame) swap.

---

## Step 1 — Power Off & Drain Adhesive Heat

1. Power off the device (**Settings → General → Shut Down**).
2. Remove the **two bottom Pentalobe P2 screws** (one each side of Lightning port).
3. Apply heat for **90–120 seconds** around the perimeter with a heat gun on low (~65 °C)
   or a heated iOpener pad. The adhesive must be warm to prevent cracking the glass.

> ⚠ **Warning:** Do not exceed 80 °C or you risk damaging the battery.

---

## Step 2 — Open the Display

1. Attach a suction cup to the **lower-left corner** of the screen.
2. Pull firmly upward while inserting a metal pick into the gap at the **bottom edge**.
3. Slide the pick **left** along the bottom, then **up the left side**, then **across the top**.
4. **Stop at the right side.** The Face ID flex cable exits at the top-right — do not pry here.
5. Lift the screen at a shallow angle (~70°) hinging from the right side like a book.

---

## Step 3 — Disconnect Battery

Before touching any connectors, disconnect the battery:

1. Remove the **two Y000 screws** from the battery connector bracket.
2. Lift the bracket off.
3. Use a plastic spudger to gently pop the battery flex connector upward.

---

## Step 4 — Disconnect Display Cables

1. Remove the **two Y000 screws** from the display shield plate.
2. Lift the shield plate off with tweezers.
3. Disconnect the **OLED connector** and the **digitiser connector** — both click off with a spudger.
4. Disconnect the **Front Camera / Face ID cable** last — this is the most delicate cable.

> ⚠ Face ID is calibrated at the factory. If the front camera module is damaged, Face ID
> **cannot** be restored by a third-party repair shop.

---

## Step 5 — Install New Display

1. Verify the replacement assembly matches the original (check connector positions).
2. Transfer the **Front Camera / Earpiece / Face ID module** from the original to the new frame
   (required to preserve Face ID).
3. Connect the display cables in reverse order: Front Camera → Digitiser → OLED.
4. Re-install the shield plate and screws.
5. Reconnect the battery connector.

---

## Step 6 — Test Before Sealing

1. Power on the phone.
2. Verify: Touch responsiveness across all 4 corners, Face ID enrollment, True Tone toggle,
   ambient light sensor, earpiece audio, front camera.
3. Check for backlight bleed at max brightness in a dark room.

---

## Step 7 — Seal & Close

1. Apply new **adhesive gasket strips** around the inside perimeter of the display.
2. Press the display into the frame starting from the top hinge, then press down the sides.
3. Apply firm pressure around all edges for 30 seconds.
4. Re-insert the two Pentalobe P2 bottom screws.

---

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Touch unresponsive in one corner | Cable not fully seated | Reopen and reseat digitiser connector |
| White horizontal lines | Damaged OLED cable | Replace display (cable bent) |
| Face ID not available | Camera module damaged | Source genuine Apple module |
| Screen flickers at low brightness | Non-genuine OLED panel | Use OEM-grade display |
| Water not repelled | Old adhesive strips reused | Replace adhesive gasket |
