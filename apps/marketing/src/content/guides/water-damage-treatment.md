## Overview

Modern phones rated IP67/IP68 can resist brief submersion, but prolonged water exposure,
saltwater, or liquid other than clean water can corrode internal components within hours.
Speed is critical — the first 30 minutes determine the outcome.

---

## Triage Priority Matrix

| Liquid Type | Risk Level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Clean fresh water | Medium | 48-hour dry, IPA clean |
| Rainwater | Medium-High | IPA clean mandatory |
| Saltwater / seawater | Critical | IPA clean within 1 hour or board scrap |
| Coffee / juice / soft drink | High | IPA ultrasonic clean |
| Toilet water | High | IPA + sanitise |

> ⚠ **Saltwater is the most destructive** — chloride ions actively corrode copper traces.
> A phone submerged in the sea has roughly 6–8 hours before board corrosion is irreversible.

---

## Step 1 — Immediate Actions (First 5 Minutes)

1. **Remove from liquid immediately.**
2. **DO NOT turn it on** — this can short circuit the board.
3. If the phone is on: **hold power button → Emergency power off** (do not swipe to normal shutdown which takes longer).
4. Remove the SIM tray and SIM card.
5. Pat the exterior completely dry with a clean towel.
6. Blow out ports (USB-C, speakers, SIM slot) with short bursts of canned air.

---

## Step 2 — Disassembly for Drying (Workshop Only)

For best results, open the device and remove the battery immediately:

1. Follow the device-specific battery replacement guide (see guides for iPhone, Samsung, etc.).
2. Remove the battery as quickly as possible — a charged battery in a wet phone accelerates corrosion.
3. Inspect the board for **corrosion indicators** (green or white crystalline deposits).

**If corrosion is visible:** proceed to Step 3 (IPA clean) immediately.
**If no corrosion:** proceed to Step 4 (drying).

---

## Step 3 — Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA) Clean

> Only needed if corrosion is visible, or for high-risk liquids (saltwater, sugary drinks).

1. Using a soft brush, apply **99% IPA** to the affected areas of the board.
2. Scrub gently in circular motions to dislodge corrosion deposits.
3. Focus on: charging port board, battery connector area, CPU/RAM area (often near SIM slot).
4. Allow IPA to evaporate fully (IPA evaporates in ~30 seconds — it is safe to use liberally).
5. Inspect under magnification for any remaining corrosion. Repeat if necessary.
6. For severe cases, an **ultrasonic cleaner** with IPA will reach between IC pins.

> 💡 IPA works because it displaces water, dissolves corrosive deposits, and evaporates
> without leaving residue. Never use tap water for rinsing — it adds more minerals.

---

## Step 4 — Drying

**Best method — desiccant:**
1. Place the open device (battery removed) in a sealed zip-lock bag with **10–15 silica gel packets**.
2. Leave for **48–72 hours** at room temperature.

**Acceptable method — air drying:**
1. Place the open device near a fan (not a heater) for 24–48 hours.
2. Ensure airflow reaches the charging port and speaker openings.

**Last resort — rice:**
Rice is a myth — it absorbs almost no moisture from electronics and leaves starch dust
in ports. Use silica gel or air drying instead.

---

## Step 5 — Reassembly & Test

1. After 48 hours drying, reinstall the battery.
2. Before powering on, visually inspect all connectors for residual moisture.
3. Connect to power — check if it charges (voltage indicator should appear within 10 seconds).
4. Power on and test: display, touch, cameras, cellular, Wi-Fi, speaker, microphone.
5. Run a **full diagnostics** check.

## Success Rate Estimates

| Exposure Duration | Liquid | Clean Water Treatment Only | IPA + Ultrasonic |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 30 min | Fresh water | 85% | 95% |
| < 30 min | Saltwater | 40% | 75% |
| 1–4 hours | Fresh water | 60% | 80% |
| 1–4 hours | Saltwater | 10% | 40% |
| 4+ hours | Any | < 20% | 30–50% |

> These are industry estimates. Actual results depend on device condition and whether
> the phone was powered on while wet.
